Connor O'Donnell

I am a student of International Relations at Warwick University,  also holding a Bachelor's degree in Politics and International Relations from Goldsmiths University of London. My professional background includes serving as a Web Content Editor at Europe's largest supplier of cinema and film production equipment, as well as contributing to 'Outside the Bubble' news as a content writer/reporter, analysing and communicating diverse perspectives of UK current affairs.

I have a robust foundation in political theory, with a keen interest in theories such as Realism, The English School and Utilitarianism, as well as the historical foundations that underpin them. Originating from the viewing of a Vice Media YouTube video as an early teenager, my entry into political academia began with a fascination with nuclear politics, North Korea and as an onlooker into the 'Creeping Border' of Russia and Georgia, which would not only become the subject of my undergraduate dissertation but also be the focus of a self-funded research trip undertaken nearly a decade later. Beyond these specialised focuses, my broad knowledge of Politics and International Relations extends to the domestic and international politics of most nations, as part of a commitment to staying abreast of current affairs and my dedication to a comprehensive understanding of International Relations and the field of Political Science.

Beyond academia and professional endeavours, my interests and skills paint a more comprehensive picture of myself. Proficient in writing and research, I delve into diverse topics with a keen eye for detail as well as genuine curiosity. I possess HTML/CSS coding proficiency as evidenced through the creation and hosting of this website, as well as a small YouTube channel, showcasing my interest in technology and its seamless merging with content creation and delivery. My fascination towards varied cultures helps foster a global perspective when paired with an interest in history, politics and current affairs, as well as dedication towards field-work and travelling - key in gaining first-hand experience to afford myself a well-rounded and informed foundation for shaping impartial and insightful opinions.
